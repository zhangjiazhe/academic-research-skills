# Graduate Thesis Introduction Rules

## Position in Workflow

1. Write the Introduction only after the main chapters and conclusion chapter are complete.
2. The Introduction must be based on the already completed content of the thesis.
3. The literature review must coordinate with the reference rules and citation-placement rules.
4. Keep the Introduction forward-looking rather than conclusion-like.
5. Do not present this thesis's method before explaining why the problem requires it.

## Overall Structure

The Introduction should contain the following major parts in order:

1. Background section
2. Literature review section(s)
3. Current-research summary by problem/chapter
4. Research-content section

## Background Requirements

1. Use five to six paragraphs to introduce the overall background of the thesis.
2. The background must explain:
   - the macro problem context
   - the specific problem context studied in this thesis
   - the significance of the research problem
3. This background portion must contain more than 10 literature citations in total.
4. If the verified source base is insufficient for this citation density, request upstream `deep-research` support before drafting the full background.

## Literature Review Requirements

1. After the background, write the literature review.
2. For every major problem / chapter in this thesis, create one dedicated literature-review subsection.
3. Every chapter involved in the thesis must have its own literature-review subsection.
4. The literature review must be written as a research trajectory in time order or theory-progress order, not as a list of isolated cases.
5. Start with technical progress:
   - earlier techniques became insufficient
   - newer techniques emerged
   - newer techniques still have limitations that this thesis addresses
6. Organize the literature by:
   - inheritance
   - critique
   - innovation
7. Make the development route of the field clear.
8. Extract:
   - turning points
   - disputes
   - unresolved problems
   - emerging trends
9. Explain the significance and urgency of each research problem, and how these problems guide the research design.
10. Distinguish this thesis clearly from prior work, especially in how the work is carried out differently or more effectively.
11. Before explaining why a certain problem needs a certain method, do not present this thesis's method in the review.
12. When multiple solution types address the same problem, a comparison table may be used for clarity.

## Literature Review Writing Guidance

The review should reflect the author's understanding of the field's research logic rather than merely listing cases.

A usable pattern is:

`Xxx问题一直是xxx。为此，XXX首先讨论了xxx问题，构建了xxx模型/设计了xxx算法，结合xxx开展了实例分析，验证了xxx。该研究首次进行了xxx，但xxx。为此，xxx进一步进行了xxx。……。但他们未关注Xxx，使得xxx。`

This pattern is optional, not mandatory.

## Current-Research Summary by Problem

1. After the literature review, write one section summarizing the state of current research and its limitations.
2. This summary must be organized by the problems / chapters of this thesis.
3. Every identified limitation must land on this thesis rather than remain generic.
4. The purpose is to show how each chapter's research task grows out of an existing gap or deficiency.

## Research Content Requirements

1. After the current-research summary, write the research-content section.
2. Start with one overall sentence such as:
   - `围绕（针对）xx问题，研究了xx。`
3. In that overall sentence:
   - do not reveal the technical path too early
   - explain why this research path is chosen
   - explain the intended purpose
4. Then expand the research content in ordered paragraphs or ordered points.
5. Use layered expressions such as:
   - `分析了xx`
   - `形成了xx`
   - `验证了xx`
   - `建立了xx`
   - `研究了xx`
   - `构建了xx`
6. If the structure is complex, use ordered forms such as:
   - `首先……其次……最后……`
   - `①……②……③……`
7. The research-content section describes what the thesis will carry out; it must not write the final conclusions.

## Reference Coordination Rules

1. The Introduction must obey the thesis-level reference rules and citation-placement rules together.
2. Do not fill citation positions with guessed or unverifiable sources.
3. If the background or literature review needs more verified references, expand the source base through `deep-research` first.
