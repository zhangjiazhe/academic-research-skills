# Graduate Thesis Shared Style Rules

## Scope

These rules are the shared whole-thesis constraints for the `毕业论文` route.
They apply across all chapters, the conclusion chapter, the Introduction, figures, tables, equations, references, wording, and citation placement.

## Whole-Thesis Control Rule

1. The entire thesis must continuously obey the same rule groups:
   - title rules
   - reference rules
   - formatting rules
   - figure / table / formula rules
   - citation-placement rules
   - wording rules
2. Reference rules and citation-placement rules must be used together.
3. All sentences must serve the overall research subject.
4. Do not drift away from the main line of the thesis.

## Title Rules

### Level 1 Headings

1. The heading should express the chapter theme clearly.
2. The research object should be identifiable.
3. A verb may be used where it improves clarity.

### Level 2 Headings

1. The heading is used for module division.
2. A verb may be used where helpful.
3. Innovation-related headings should explicitly surface key constructs, such as `xxx模型构建`.
4. The research object usually does not need to be written if the meaning remains clear.

### Level 3 Headings

1. The heading is used for concrete steps.
2. It must contain an action verb.
3. The research object usually does not need to be written if the meaning remains clear.

### Forward-Term Consistency

Important innovation terms, conclusion terms, and major professional terms should appear in earlier headings where appropriate, so that readers can trace final innovation and conclusion terminology back through the thesis structure.

## Reference Rules

1. Keep references in a dedicated `.bib` file.
2. Search for real journal papers and verify that the papers actually exist.
3. Never fabricate papers, venues, years, or other citation metadata.
4. Do not list content unrelated to the thesis.
5. Chinese references should preferentially come from `卓越期刊` level or above.
6. English references should preferably be:
   - non-OA when stronger alternatives exist
   - non-MDPI when stronger alternatives exist
   - preferably Q2 or above
   - or IF > 5 where appropriate
7. If the section lacks enough verified sources, request upstream `deep-research` support before continuing the citation-heavy section.

## Formatting Rules

### Figures

1. Under single-page layout, unify figure length to 9 cm.
2. Use JPG or PNG.
3. Use Songti / Times New Roman according to the paper language and layout.

### Tables

1. Experimental-result decimals must be unified to four decimal places.

### Whole Paper

1. Title: Xiao Er, Songti, bold
2. Level 1 heading: size 4, Songti, bold
3. Level 2 heading: Xiao 4, Heiti, bold
4. Level 3 heading: Xiao 4, Songti, bold
5. Do not use level-4 headings or deeper.
6. Enumerated points must use `（1）`, `（2）`, ...
7. Process-model content must use `step 1: ...`
8. If many symbols are used, prepare a dedicated symbol table that is convenient and clear.

## Figure / Table / Formula Rules

1. Figure and table titles must foreground content, so readers can broadly understand what is being studied.
2. A strong title should contain at least several 5W dimensions:
   - who
   - where
   - what
   - how
   - why
3. Every figure must have textual explanation in the thesis body.
4. Subfigure names should be marked after the figure title where relevant.
5. Parameters, environments, and settings may be stated in figure or table titles when useful.
6. Equation size and formatting should remain consistent throughout the thesis.
7. Running-text equations should use direct English symbols so line spacing remains consistent.

## Citation-Placement Rules

1. Do not cite places with no substantive academic content.
2. Do not cite trivial qualitative wording or common knowledge that everyone in the field already knows.
3. Every applied method must be cited.
4. Every cited standard must be cited at the specific point of use.
5. Every selected numeric parameter, threshold, or scale basis must be cited.
6. When formulas and second-level figure titles are cited, the references must correspond concretely to visible content in the thesis.
7. If a figure or formula is mentioned in the text, it must actually appear in the thesis.

## Wording Rules

1. Abbreviations and acronyms must be defined at first appearance in:
   - the abstract
   - the main text
   - the first figure or table part where they appear
2. Preferred expressions include:
   - `分析了xx`
   - `形成了xx`
   - `验证了xx`
   - `建立了xx`
   - `研究了xx`
   - `构建了xx`
3. Avoid `可能` when it signals unsupported guessing.
4. Avoid `例如` when it weakens completeness and makes the statement selective.
5. Write `本文` / `本章` / `本节`, not `本研究`.
6. In Chinese, reduce weak verbs such as `分析` and `选取`; prefer stronger wording such as `引入`、`研究`、`建立`、`构建`.
7. In Results, Discussion, and Conclusion, restrained wording such as `有效` and `准确` is acceptable when supported.
8. During the research process before results are established, do not self-evaluate the work. In Method and research-path description, avoid:
   - `系统`
   - `全面`
   - `综合`
   - `有效`
9. Reduce qualitative prose. Depth should come from:
   - numbers
   - figures
   - tables
   - formulas
   - multi-layer study of the research object
10. All sentences must serve the overall thesis topic; avoid disconnected sentence chains.
11. Basic domain knowledge does not need excessive explanation.
12. Emphasize the formulas, formula prerequisites, reports, and technical content actually needed by this thesis.
13. Do not over-explain textbook or standards content that the field already knows.
14. Keep others' formulas concise; expand only the part actually used by the thesis.
15. Compress purely descriptive language derived from simple statistical summaries.
16. Avoid report-style, science-popularization, and generic filler prose.
17. Avoid repeating background, problem, and significance too many times across the thesis.

## Whole-Thesis Discipline Rules

1. Use real, verified references only.
2. Do not fabricate references, parameters, standards, formulas, or citation locations.
3. If references are missing, outdated, or cannot be verified, request upstream `deep-research` support before continuing the section.
4. All wording, references, formatting, figure/table/formula rules, and citation-placement rules apply to the whole thesis, not just one chapter.
