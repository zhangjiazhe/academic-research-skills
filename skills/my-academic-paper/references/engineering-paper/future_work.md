# Engineering Paper Future Work Rules

1. Start this section by first critiquing the limitations of the current study. State what the present work did not yet solve, where its scope remains narrow, or what research-level weakness still remains.
2. After the limitation statement, transition into future outlook. The outlook must grow directly out of those stated limitations rather than appear as an unrelated wish list.
3. Future work must stay at the research level and originate from this study's limitations and next-step questions.
4. Do not reduce future work to generic data collection or fieldwork tasks.
5. Good future-work directions include:
   - research transfer to similar sites or similar objects
   - engineering practice and real deployment workflow
   - model updates and comparison with newer models
   - larger and more diverse multi-source data types
   - concrete implementation path under real policy or operational constraints
6. Do not write hypothetical scenarios casually; future work should describe feasible research directions rather than unsupported assumptions.
7. Each future-work point should be concrete rather than vague, and should connect back to the current study.
