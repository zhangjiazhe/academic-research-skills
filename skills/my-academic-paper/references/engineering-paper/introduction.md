# Engineering Paper Introduction Rules

## Workflow

After the other sections are completed:

1. Write two background paragraphs:
   - one macro-background paragraph explaining the broad problem context
   - one focused background paragraph explaining the concrete problem studied here and its significance
2. These background paragraphs must contain 3-5 literature citations.
3. Both background paragraphs must connect explicitly back to this study rather than stop at generic background description.
4. Add one clear significance sentence that states why the specific problem studied here matters for the present paper.
5. Then write the literature review section.
6. Based on the literature review, write one current-research-gap summary paragraph, and every identified gap must land on this study.
7. Based on those gaps, write the research content section:
   - first, one overall sentence such as "围绕（针对）xx问题，研究了xx。"
   - do not reveal the detailed technical path too early
   - explain the reason for choosing this research path and its intended purpose
   - then expand hierarchically using formulations such as `分析了xx、形成了xx、验证了xx、建立了xx、研究了xx、构建了xx`
8. The research content section must describe what will be carried out, not write the final conclusions.
9. After the research content, write the innovation points.
10. If the background paragraphs, literature review, or innovation-point evidence need additional verified references, request an upstream `deep-research` search before drafting those citation-dependent passages.

## Constraints

1. The introduction must include:
   - macro background paragraph
   - focused problem background paragraph
   - literature review
   - current-research-gap summary
   - research content
   - innovation points
2. The two background paragraphs must include 3-5 citations in total.
3. The two background paragraphs must both lead back to the present paper, and must contain one explicit significance sentence tied to this study.
4. Every identified gap must connect directly to this study rather than stay generic.
5. Research content must be forward-looking and must not write final conclusions.
6. Innovation points must focus on technical innovation in research object, method, solved problem, and achieved effect, with quantitative support where possible.
7. Innovation-point wording should avoid terms such as `一套`, `全面`, and `系统`.
8. Innovation points should not treat simple method stacking as a new method.
9. Innovation-point blocks should stay concise, roughly around five lines when rendered.
10. Do not fill citation slots with guessed or unverified references; use user-provided sources or sources returned through `deep-research`.
